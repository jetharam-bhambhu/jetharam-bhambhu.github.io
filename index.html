About

Background

I completed a BS–MS in Mathematics at IISER Pune. My master's thesis, supervised by Prof. Naveen Garg and Prof. Amit Kumar at IIT Delhi, studied approximation algorithms for a submodular generalization of the Interval Stabbing Problem.

From October 2026, I will be a PhD student in Prof. Christoph Buchheim's group at TU Dortmund. My doctoral research will focus on bilevel optimization under uncertainty.

Research

Research Interests

My primary interest is in algorithms for computationally hard optimization problems — understanding when efficient approximate solutions exist and when they do not. I work mainly with linear programming relaxations and rounding techniques. My doctoral research will extend to bilevel optimization under uncertainty.
