About

A little about me

I am a mathematician turned algorithm designer, broadly interested in how well we can efficiently solve hard optimization problems. My work sits at the intersection of combinatorial optimization, linear programming, and the theory of NP-hardness — figuring out which problems admit good approximations and which ones provably do not.

I completed my BS–MS Dual Degree in Mathematics at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER), Pune. My master's thesis — supervised by Prof. Naveen Garg and Prof. Amit Kumar at IIT Delhi — studied approximation algorithms for a submodular variant of the Interval Stabbing Problem. Along the way, I derived an 8-approximation for the Multilevel Aggregation Problem with Holding and Delay Costs via LP rounding, and established APX-hardness for a restricted subclass.

In October 2026, I will join Prof. Christoph Buchheim's group at TU Dortmund as a PhD researcher, funded through the ALMOA Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions fellowship programme. My PhD will focus on bilevel optimization under uncertainty — a rich area where algorithmic, structural, and complexity-theoretic questions converge.

Research

What I work on

I am drawn to problems that are computationally intractable in the worst case, and to the question of how much efficiency we can recover through clever algorithm design. My primary tools are linear programming relaxations, LP rounding schemes, and reductions that characterize the limits of approximability. Going forward, my PhD will explore bilevel optimization — problems where two agents optimize sequentially with coupled objectives — under uncertainty.

1
Approximation Algorithms
Designing polynomial-time algorithms that find near-optimal solutions to NP-hard problems, with provable worst-case guarantees. My thesis produced an 8-approximation for a multilevel scheduling problem via LP rounding.
2
Submodular Optimization
Algorithmic and hardness questions for optimization over submodular objectives — a natural class capturing diminishing returns, which arises in scheduling, network design, and machine learning.
3
Linear Programming & NP-hardness
Understanding when LP relaxations achieve the best possible approximation ratios, and when they cannot — through integrality gap analysis, APX-hardness reductions, and inapproximability arguments.
4
Bilevel Optimization under Uncertainty · Upcoming PhD
Problems where a leader and a follower optimize sequentially, and the environment is uncertain. This area sits at the intersection of combinatorial optimization, robust optimization, and game theory.
