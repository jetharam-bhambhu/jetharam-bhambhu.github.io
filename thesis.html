Master's Thesis

Approximation Algorithms for Submodular Generalizations of the Interval Stabbing Problem

Supervisors: Prof. Naveen Garg & Prof. Amit Kumar (IIT Delhi)

Institution: Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER), Pune & Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Delhi

Defense Date: May 2026

Download PDF (Thesis) Download Slides

Abstract

The Interval Stabbing Problem is a classical combinatorial optimization problem where the goal is to select a minimum-cost set of points to pierce or "stab" a given set of intervals on a line. In this thesis, we study a generalization of this problem where the stabbing costs are modeled via submodular functions, capturing the realistic economic scenario of diminishing returns or joint economies of scale.

Under a submodular cost structure, the traditional greedy and LP-rounding approaches for interval stabbing do not directly apply due to the non-additivity of costs. We present approximation algorithms for this submodular interval stabbing problem under various constraint structures. By leveraging LP relaxations with submodular objectives, rounding schemes, and primal-dual methods, we develop polynomial-time approximation algorithms with provable performance guarantees. We also explore the hardness of approximation for these variants, delineating the boundaries of what can be efficiently approximated.

Key Contributions

  • Framework Formulation: Formulated the Submodular Interval Stabbing Problem (SISP), unifying several interval covering models under a submodular cost framework.
  • LP-Rounding Algorithms: Developed a novel linear programming relaxation and corresponding rounding scheme that yields provable approximation factors.
  • Primal-Dual Techniques: Designed primal-dual approximation algorithms to handle specific subclasses of interval structures with submodular cost sharing.
  • Hardness Bounds: Established computational complexity and inapproximability limits for SISP, proving matching lower bounds for certain classes of submodular objectives.
